An important feature of atomic theory is its explanation of a chemical reaction as a rearrangement of the atoms of substances. The reaction of sodium metal with chlorine gas described in the chapter opening involves the rearrangement of the atoms of sodium and chlorine to give the new combination of atoms in sodium chloride.
