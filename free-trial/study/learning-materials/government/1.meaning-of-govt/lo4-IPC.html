1.7 IMPORTANCE OF STUDYING GOVERNMENT ### 1. Helping in knowing the type of system of government: The study of government helps in knowing the types and different systems of government operating in the world. ### 2. To widen people’s knowledge: The study of government helps to widen people’s knowledge about administration and other institutions involved in governance. ### 3. Political education: Government educates the people of a country on the political life or affairs of the country. ### 4. Opportunity for leadership: The opportunity is provided for people through the study of government to become future political leaders. ### 5. Conflict resolution: Through government, an opportunity is provided for people to know how conflicts originate and are resolved. ### 6. Government provides the opportunity to know: Government provides the opportunity for people to know their rights, duties and obligations as citizens. ### 7. Prevention of mistakes of past leaders: The study of government helps to prevent mistakes of some past political leaders from being repeated. ### 8. Promotion of the spirit of nationalism and patriotism: These are incubated into the lives of the people. ### 9. Decision-making: The study of government equally opens the eyes of the people for the need to take part in political decision-making of a country. ### 10. The study of government: This enables us to differentiate between a legitimate government and the one that is not. A legitimate government must have the support of the people. ### 11. Career opportunities: It provides career opportunities for those who study it. ### 12. Relationship: Study of government makes it possible for an improved relationship between the ruler and the ruled. This is because it opens the understanding of the government to undertake actions and policies meant for the general well-being of the society. ### 13. Political occurrences and developments: The study of government helps in keeping abreast of national and global political occurrences and developments. ## 14. International relations: It deepens the understanding of international relations and a state’s commitment to international organisations. 15. Acquisition of knowledge: It provides knowledge of the political history of the state. 16. Provision of solutions: It provides solutions to socio-political and economic problems by the introduction of programmes and policies and best practices in other political environments. ## 17. Leadership training: The study of government offers the opportunity for leadership training. ## 18. Political issues: It teaches us to be objective in our analysis of political issues. ## 19. Political culture: It helps the individual to develop a political culture. ## 20. Comparative politics: The study also deals with comparative analysis of government of other states FUNCTIONS OF GOVERNMENT The followingare the major functions ofmodern govemment. 1. Law-making: Government is responsible for making laws in a country. Laws are made to ensure peace and order in the society. The legislature is the organ of government responsible for law-making. 2. Provision of social amenities: Amenities likeelectricity, potable water, health centre, etc. are provided by the government. These are necessities for human existence. 3. Maintenance of law and order: The maintenance of law and order is the responsibilityofgovernment. This function is usually performed by government agencies like the police. 4. Defence of the country: Government is responsible for defending the country against external aggression. It is the duty of the armed forces of a country to maintain the territorial integrity of the state. 5. Protection of lives and property: Government is also responsible for protection of lives and property. For example, any person can seek the police protection if there is the need for it. 6. Provision of employment: Jobs are also provided for the citizens. Thisgoesalong waytowards improving the welfare of the people. 7. Formulation and implementation of policies: Formulation and implementation of policies are carried out to ensure th well-being of the people. The executive arm of goverment performs this function. 8. Promotion of economic activities: The promotion of economic activities is done through the provision ofinfrastructure,e.g. roads, railways, airports, etc. and by encouraging local and foreign investors. 9. External affairs function: Government maintains good relations with other countries. The Head of State and the Ministry of External Affairs perform this function in Nigeria. Diplomats who represent their countries in other countries further this noble role. 10. Administration of justice: The judiciary is responsible for the administration of justice. It settles disputes between individuals and between individuals and the government. 11. Maintenance of unity: Uniting all ethnic groups, sections and people of different religious beliefs resulting in a harmonious and peaceful community. 12. Settlement of disputes: Government settles disputes between groups, individuals and government. The judicial arm of government performs this noble function. 13. Economic functions: It ensures economic growth, boosts industrialisation as well as agricultural development and equally regulates and controls the economy. 14. Political functions: Government establishes machinery for smooth and peaceful change of government through the conduct of regular, free and fair elections. 15. Raising of public revenue: This is done through imposition of taxes, fines, fees, duties from import, export, etc. in order to get funds for its services. ## 16. Fundamental human rights: The government is duty-bound to respect and protect the fundamental rights of man as entrenched in the constitution. ## 17. Human rights: The fundamental human rights, e.g. right to life, property, etc. are entrenched in the constitution. It is the responsibility of the government to see to the protection of these rights. ## 18. Revenue generation: The provision of infrastructure, e.g. roads, airports, railways, and other social amenities, hospital, water, etc. is made possible through enough generation of revenue by the government. ## 19. Protection of national interest: In pursuing foreign policy objectives, the people’s economic and social well-being should be paramount. This is one of the functions of government. ## 20. Conduct of elections and population census: These functions are usually performed by government agencies/boards, e.g. INEC, census board, etc. CHARACTERISTICS OF GOVERNMENT The following are the attributes of government as an institution ofthe state: Law: It is a guide to the activities, beliefs and behaviour of the citizens. These are laws made by the government and entrenched in the constitution. The actions of those in government, their limitations, rights, duties and obligations of citizens are all outlined in the constitution. Political power: The political power which the government has is in consonance with the people's will to obey laws and policies made by the state. 3. Revenue: Government needs enough revenue for the enormous task facing it. 4. Work-force: The civil service possesses adequate personnel for policy-making and implementation, interpretation and punishment ofoffenders. 5. People's legitimate support: A government should be able to earn for itself legitimate support from the people and this produces peace and stability in a system. 6. Welfare services: Provision of some essential services, e.g. water, electricity, housing, etc. should be made to improve people's well-being. This is essential for any responsible government. 7.Periodic change: No government is permanent, especially one that is based on people's participation (elective government), hence the adoption of periodic elections. In some systems, it could be either five or four years to change agoverment. 8. Protection: It is incumbent on the government to provide enough protection and security to the people.
