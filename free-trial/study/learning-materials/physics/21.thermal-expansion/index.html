Thermal Expansion (a) Solids (i) definition and determination of linear, volume and area expansivities; (ii) effects and applications, e.g. expansion in building strips and railway lines; (iii) relationship between different expansivities. (b) Liquids (i) volume expansivity; (ii) real and apparent expansivities; (iii) determination of volume expansivity; (iv) anomalous expansion of water. Candidates should be able to: i. determine linear and volume expansivities; ii. assess the effects and applications of thermal expansivities; iii. determine the relationship between different expansivities; iv. determine volume, apparent, and real expansivities of liquids; v. analyse the anomalous expansion of water.
