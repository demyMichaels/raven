2.4 Electronic Configuration

How electrons fill the orbitals (roommate rules)
🛏️
Think of orbitals like dorm rooms: Each room fits two people, but they have to have opposite spins (like facing away from each other). If there are multiple rooms (orbitals), people spread out first before sharing.

The Three Rules of Electron Housing

1️⃣ Aufbau principle: Fill the lowest energy orbitals first (1s before 2s, 2s before 2p, etc.)

2️⃣ Pauli exclusion principle: Each orbital holds max 2 electrons, and they must have opposite spins (↑↓).

3️⃣ Hund's rule: When filling degenerate orbitals (like 2px, 2py, 2pz), put one electron in each first (all same spin), then double up.

Why Noble Gases are "Noble"

👑 Helium: 1s² – first shell completely full (2 electrons).

👑 Neon: 1s²2s²2p⁶ – second shell completely full (8 electrons – an octet).

👑 Argon: 1s²2s²2p⁶3s²3p⁶ – third shell full (another octet).

👑 Krypton: 1s²2s²2p⁶3s²3p⁶4s²3d¹⁰4p⁶ – full shells.

These filled shells are super stable. That's why noble gases don't react much – they're already happy. Atoms bond to achieve this same stability – by gaining, losing, or sharing electrons to get a full outer shell (octet rule).
