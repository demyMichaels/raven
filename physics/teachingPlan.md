The best way to learn physics is to constantly connect new topics to old ones.


When studying 10 🎯 Projectiles, link it back to 09 📈 Linear Motion.

When studying 26 🔥 Quantity of Heat, link it back to 16 ⚡ Work, Energy & Power (Heat is a form of energy).

When studying 46 ⚡ Current Electricity, link it back to 07 ⚖️ Newton's Laws (Electrons moving due to an electric "force") 
and 16 ⚡ Work, Energy & Power (Electrical energy).

When studying 49 ⚡ Force on Conductor, you are literally combining 46 (Current Electricity) and 48 (Magnets & Fields) 



If a concept in 43 (Electrostatics) feels abstract, remember it's just 07 (Forces) again. You already learned about forces; now you're just learning about a new type of force.




4. A Suggested Learning Path
This list is perfect for a spiral learning approach:


Phase 1: The Fundamentals (1-9). 
Get comfortable with the basics of measurement, motion, and forces.

Phase 2: Core Concepts (16, 23-27). 
Understand Energy and its forms (Heat, Work).

Phase 3: Waves (33-42). 
A self-contained but crucial module.

Phase 4: The Big One - Electricity & Magnetism (43-53). 
Tackle this when you're confident with Forces and Energy.
