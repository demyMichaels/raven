Core Concepts

Load (L)

The resistance or weight the machine overcomes. Measured in Newtons (N).

L = MA × E

Effort (E)

The force applied to the machine to overcome the load.

E = L/MA

Key Formulas

Examples

Inclined Plane 30°
Plane at 30°, efficiency 60%. Force for 120N load?

VR = 1/sin30° = 2

MA = 0.6 × 2 = 1.2

E = 120/1.2 = 100N

Wheel & Axle R=80cm, r=10cm
Efficiency 85%, effort 1200N. Load?

VR = 80/10 = 8

MA = 0.85 × 8 = 6.8

L = 6.8 × 1200 = 8160N

Pulleys 6 pulleys
Efficiency 40%, load 120N. Effort?

VR = 6

MA = 0.4 × 6 = 2.4

E = 120/2.4 = 50N

Screw Jack p=0.3cm, r=21cm
Find VR

VR = 2πr/p

= (2π×21)/0.3

= 140π

What is Mechanical Advantage?

MA = Load / Effort

The ratio of load to effort. Shows force multiplication.

Quick Tips

VR is constant

Depends only on geometry, not friction

Common mistake

Use RADIUS, not diameter for wheel/axle

Mass vs Load

Load in Newtons: multiply kg × 10

Efficiency

Always < 100% due to friction
