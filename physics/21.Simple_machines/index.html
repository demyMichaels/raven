Simple Machines

Discover the six fundamental machines that make work easier

What Are Simple Machines?

Simple machines are basic mechanical devices that change the direction or magnitude of a force. They are the building blocks of every complex machine - from bicycles to cranes to rocket engines.

The six classic simple machines are: Lever, Pulley, Wheel and Axle, Inclined Plane, Wedge, and Screw.

⚖️ The Lever

A lever is a rigid bar that pivots around a fixed point called the fulcrum. It multiplies force or distance depending on where the fulcrum is placed.

Three classes of levers:

  • First Class: Fulcrum between effort and load (seesaw, crowbar)
  • Second Class: Load between fulcrum and effort (wheelbarrow)
  • Third Class: Effort between fulcrum and load (fishing rod)
MA = Distance to effort ÷ Distance to load

🔄 The Pulley

A pulley is a wheel with a groove that holds a rope. Pulleys can change the direction of force and multiply force when used in combinations.

Types: Fixed pulley (changes direction), Movable pulley (multiplies force), Block and tackle (combination)

MA = Number of ropes supporting the load

⚙️ Wheel and Axle

A wheel attached to a smaller axle. When you turn the wheel, the axle turns with less force but shorter distance.

Examples: Steering wheel, doorknob, screwdriver, bicycle pedals

MA = Radius of wheel ÷ Radius of axle

📐 Inclined Plane

An inclined plane is a sloping surface connecting a lower level to a higher level. It allows you to raise a load by applying a smaller force over a longer distance.

Examples: Ramps, slides, sloping roads

MA = Length of incline ÷ Height

🔪 The Wedge

A wedge is two inclined planes back-to-back. It converts a downward force into splitting forces sideways.

Examples: Axe, knife, chisel, door wedge, nail

MA = Length of wedge ÷ Thickness

🧵 The Screw

A screw is an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder. It converts rotational force into linear force.

Examples: Screws, bolts, jar lids, drill bits, vises

MA = (2π × radius) ÷ pitch
pitch = distance between threads

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Simple machines don't reduce work – they trade force for distance (or distance for force)
  • Mechanical Advantage (MA) = Load/Effort – tells you how many times your force is multiplied
  • The six simple machines are: lever, pulley, wheel & axle, inclined plane, wedge, and screw
  • Levers have 3 classes based on fulcrum, load, and effort positions
  • Compound machines are combinations of two or more simple machines (bicycle, car engine, scissors)
