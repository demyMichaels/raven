Friction
(i) static and dynamic friction;
(ii) coefficient of limiting friction and its determination;
(iii) advantages and disadvantages of friction
(iv) reduction of friction;
(v) qualitative treatment of viscosity and terminal velocity;
(vi) Stoke’s law.


Candidates should be able to:
i. differentiate between static and
dynamic
friction;
ii. determine the coefficient of limiting
friction;
iii. compare the advantages and
disadvantages of friction;

iv. suggest ways by which friction can be
reduced;
v. analyse factors that affect viscosity and
terminal velocity;
vi. apply Stoke’s law
