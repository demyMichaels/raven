Candidates should be able to:
i. interpret the laws of reflection;
ii. determine the refractive index of glass
and liquid using Snell’s law;
iii. determination of focal length of curved
surfaces
iv. determine the refractive index using the
principle of real and apparent depth;
v. determine the conditions necessary for
total internal reflection;
vi. examine the use of periscope, prism,
binoculars, optical fibre;

vii. apply the principles of total internal
reflection to the formation of mirage;
viii. use of lens formula and ray diagrams to
solve optical numerical problems;
ix. determine the magnification of an
image;
x. calculate the refractive index of a glass
prism using minimum deviation
formula;
