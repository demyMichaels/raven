Mathematical Betting Principles

1. Fraction Comparison in Betting

Just like the example problem (arranging ‚Öó, 9/16, 34/59, 71/97), comparing betting odds requires converting fractions to decimals. In betting, fractional odds like 3/5, 9/16, or 5/2 represent the potential profit relative to your stake.

Example:
Bookmaker A offers 9/16 (0.5625)
Bookmaker B offers 3/5 (0.6000)
Bookmaker C offers 71/97 (0.7320)

Best odds: 71/97 = highest potential return per dollar wagered

2. Implied Probability

Fractional odds can be converted to implied probability using:
Probability = Denominator / (Numerator + Denominator)

Example with 9/16:
Probability = 16 / (9 + 16) = 16/25 = 64%
This means the bookmaker believes there's a 64% chance of this outcome occurring.

3. Finding Value Bets

A value bet exists when YOUR estimated probability is higher than the bookmaker's implied probability. This is where fraction comparison becomes crucial.
