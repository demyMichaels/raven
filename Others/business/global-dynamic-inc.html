Education Center

Economic Exposure vs. Exchange Risk
Economic exposure measures how exchange rate changes affect a company's future cash flows, competitive position, and ultimately value. Exchange risk is the potential loss from unfavorable exchange rate movements. Economic exposure is broader than just the accounting effects of FX changes.
Operating vs. Transaction Exposure
Transaction exposure relates to specific transactions denominated in foreign currencies (e.g., receivables, payables). Operating exposure is broader and captures how exchange rates affect overall operations, competitive position, and cash flows over time, even in the absence of foreign currency transactions.
Calculating Economic Exposure
Calculate economic exposure by analyzing how exchange rate changes affect: 1) Foreign revenue converted to domestic currency, 2) Cost structure including imported inputs, 3) Competitive position relative to foreign competitors, and 4) Future cash flows discounted to present value.
FX Risk Management Strategies
Effective strategies include: 1) Marketing strategies (pricing, product differentiation), 2) Production strategies (input sourcing, plant location), 3) Financial strategies (currency of denomination, hedging, local financing), and 4) Operational flexibility to quickly respond to exchange rate changes.

Strategic Decision Point

Year 1 Challenge: Initial Assessment and Strategy

The US dollar has strengthened against major currencies (EUR, JPY, GBP) by 8% over the past year. Your company has 50% of sales coming from international markets but 75% of assets and cost base in the US.

This creates significant economic exposure:

  • Foreign revenues are worth less when converted to USD
  • USD-based costs make our products less competitive in foreign markets
  • Competitors with different currency profiles may gain advantage

What approach will you take to address the immediate FX challenges?
