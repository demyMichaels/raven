Oxygen (O)

Electron Configuration: 1s² 2s² 2p⁴

Valence Electrons: 6 (2 from 2s and 4 from 2p)

Electron Shells: 2 shells (n=1 and n=2)

Notable: Needs 2 electrons to fill p-subshell

Type: Nonmetal, Chalcogen

Key Differences

Subshells: Mn has s, p, and d subshells; O only has s and p

Energy Levels: Mn has electrons in n=4 level; O only up to n=2

Electron Count: Mn has 25 electrons; O has 8 electrons

Stability: Mn has half-filled d-subshell; O needs 2 more electrons for stable octet

Chemical Behavior: Mn can form multiple oxidation states; O tends to gain electrons
