3. Angles of Elevation and Depression

Angles of elevation and depression are used to solve problems involving heights and distances. For example, if the angle of elevation to the top of a building is 30¬∞ and the distance to the building is 50m, the height of the building can be calculated using tan(30¬∞) = height / 50.

4. Bearings

Bearings are used to describe directions. They are measured clockwise from the north direction. For example, a bearing of 045¬∞ means 45¬∞ east of north.

5. Area of Triangles

The area of a triangle can be calculated using the formula Area = 0.5 * a * b * sin(C), where a and b are sides, and C is the included angle.

6. Sine and Cosine Graphs

The graphs of sine and cosine functions are periodic waves. The sine graph starts at 0, while the cosine graph starts at 1.
