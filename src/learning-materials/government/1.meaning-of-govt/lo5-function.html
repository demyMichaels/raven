1.6 FUNCTIONS OF GOVERNMENT The followingare the major functions ofmodern govemment. 1. Law-making: Government is responsible for making laws in a country. Laws are made to ensure peace and order in the society. The legislature is the organ of government responsible for law-making. 2. Provision of social amenities: Amenities likeelectricity, potable water, health centre, etc. are provided by the government. These are necessities for human existence. 3. Maintenance of law and order: The maintenance of law and order is the responsibilityofgovernment. This function is usually performed by government agencies like the police. 4. Defence of the country: Government is responsible for defending the country against external aggression. It is the duty of the armed forces of a country to maintain the territorial integrity of the state. 5. Protection of lives and property: Government is also responsible for protection of lives and property. For example, any person can seek the police protection if there is the need for it. 6. Provision of employment: Jobs are also provided for the citizens. Thisgoesalong waytowards improving the welfare of the people. 7. Formulation and implementation of policies: Formulation and implementation of policies are carried out to ensure th well-being of the people. The executive arm of goverment performs this function. 8. Promotion of economic activities: The promotion of economic activities is done through the provision ofinfrastructure,e.g. roads, railways, airports, etc. and by encouraging local and foreign investors. 9. External affairs function: Government maintains good relations with other countries. The Head of State and the Ministry of External Affairs perform this function in Nigeria. Diplomats who represent their countries in other countries further this noble role. 10. Administration of justice: The judiciary is responsible for the administration of justice. It settles disputes between individuals and between individuals and the government. 11. Maintenance of unity: Uniting all ethnic groups, sections and people of different religious beliefs resulting in a harmonious and peaceful community. 12. Settlement of disputes: Government settles disputes between groups, individuals and government. The judicial arm of government performs this noble function. 13. Economic functions: It ensures economic growth, boosts industrialisation as well as agricultural development and equally regulates and controls the economy. 14. Political functions: Government establishes machinery for smooth and peaceful change of government through the conduct of regular, free and fair elections. 15. Raising of public revenue: This is done through imposition of taxes, fines, fees, duties from import, export, etc. in order to get funds for its services. ## 16. Fundamental human rights: The government is duty-bound to respect and protect the fundamental rights of man as entrenched in the constitution. ## 17. Human rights: The fundamental human rights, e.g. right to life, property, etc. are entrenched in the constitution. It is the responsibility of the government to see to the protection of these rights. ## 18. Revenue generation: The provision of infrastructure, e.g. roads, airports, railways, and other social amenities, hospital, water, etc. is made possible through enough generation of revenue by the government. ## 19.
