ELECTION** **Definition:** Election is defined as the act of electing candidates to represent the people of a given country in the parliament, the executive and possibly into other arms of government as stipulated in the constitution of that particular country. For example, in the USA, Judges of the lower courts are elected. **13.3 FUNCTIONS OF ELECTION** 1. **Change of government:** Election makes it easier for the people to remove a bad government from office. 2. **Election of representatives:** It gives elected representatives the legitimate right to rule. 3. **Free choice of leaders:** The people have the free choice of electing leaders into different political offices. 4. **It ensures control and accountability:** Election provides the means of ensuring control and accountability of those who have been elected. 5. **It fosters democracy:** It creates the avenue for people to participate in the decision-making of their country. 6. **To educate the electorate:** People receive citizenship education and are also educated about their political rights. 7. **Legitimacy:** Election confers legitimacy on the elected government in power. 8. **Used as a measurement:** Results of elections can be used to measure the opinions of the people on issues of national importance. 9. **Competition:** Election promotes competition among the political parties and even candidates. Competition is healthy and it may make for efficiency. 10. **Equality of votes:** Election enhances equality of votes of citizens. It is a case of one man, one vote. 11. **Type of policies:** Election makes it possible for people to decide the type of policies that should be adopted by government through party manifestoes. 12. **Integration:** Election integrates various ethnic and political groups. 13. **Popularity:** It serves as an index to measure the popularity of both party and candidates. 14. **Political sovereignty:** Election serves as a means of ensuring that political sovereignty is vested in the people in a democracy. 15. **Good governance:** It equally helps to promote good governance and curtails dictatorial tendencies of government. **Demerits of Election** 1. **Elections make for antagonistic groups:** Elections can divide the country into antagonistic groups with different camps opposing one another. 2. **Disunity:** Division of the country for election purposes or people belonging to different political parties can cause disunity in the country. 3. **Wastage of funds:** A lot of money, materials, etc. are wasted conducting 4. **Election rigging:** Rigging may enthrone the people of dubious intentions into government. 5. **Uneven development:** The party in government may decide not to assist areas that did not vote or support them during elections. This can result in uneven development of the country. 6. **Best materials may not be involved in the administration:** They may withdraw from taking part in elections, as a result of thuggery, intimidations, victimisation, etc. that characterise elections. 7. **Marginalisation:** Elections may still marginalise some groups in the society. This may cause disaffection in the political system. **13.4 TYPES OF ELECTIONS** 1. Direct election 2. Indirect election 3. Bye-election 4. Run-off election or Second Ballot 5. Primary election 6. Referendum 7. Plebiscite 8. Supplementary and inconclusive election 9. Staggered election 10. Re-run election Discussions on the types of elections as mentioned above will be made one at a time. **13.5 DIRECT ELECTION** **Meaning:** Direct elections involve the electorate (voters) casting their votes directly in an election for candidates of their choice that will represent them either in the executive or legislature, without any interference. **Merits of Direct Election** 1. **Restoration of voters’ rights:** The rights of the electors are restored in voting for the right candidates of their choice. 2. **It is democratic:** It affords mass participation in political decision-making of the country. 3. **Equality of votes:** One man, one vote applies. It gives the electorate equality of votes in the system. 4. **Popular choice:** Direct elections reflect popular choice of the people. 5. **It enhances dedication and hard work:** Most of the candidates work very hard to defeat their political opponents in the election. 6. **Creation of opportunity to know prospective representatives:** It creates the opportunity for people to know the prospective representatives, aspiring to represent their interests in government. 7. **It is simple to operate:** Direct elections are simple and can easily be adopted in many countries of the world. **Demerits of Direct Election** 1. **Illiteracy:** Those who cannot read or write usually vote wrongly or blindly because they cannot identify party symbols and names. 2. **Problems of rigging:** Direct elections are generally characterised by malpractices. Result may at times be falsified to favour a particular candidate. 3. **Corrupt electoral officers:** Some electoral officers connive with politicians to ensure the success of a particular party or candidate. 4. **Poor turnout:** Poor turnout of voters is usually not a true reflection of the number of those who registered. 5. **Inadequate security at polling stations:** In most cases, many polling stations lack adequate security and this can lead to intimidation and harassment of voters by thugs and other miscreants. 6. **Thuggery and intimidation:** It can encourage violence, victimisation of political opponents, etc. **13.6 TYPES OF VOTING** Direct elections could be by public voting or secret balloting. Any of the voting methods may be used in electing political leaders in a country. **Secret balloting:** Voters cast their votes secretly. The voter alone makes the decision in the voting exercise. In this election, ballot papers inscribing the names of the parties and symbols, ballot box, envelope (may or may not be used), ink pad, etc. are some of the materials needed for secret voting. Also, a presiding officer, polling clerk and polling orderly are the electoral officials in charge of a polling station. There are also security agents (the police) and party agents. **Merits of Secret Balloting** 1. **It is democratic:** Secret voting preserves the rights of the voter. He makes the decision alone without coercion. 2. **It is universal:** It is more of a universal kind of voting than the public voting (open balloting). 3. **Sense of direction:** It gives the voter a sense of direction in deciding whom to vote for. 4. **Free from intimidation:** The voter is free from intimidation, harassment from his landlord, employer, etc. 5. **Freedom of choice:** It gives the voter freedom to decide for a candidate of his choice. 6. **It gives satisfaction:** The voter is satisfied with the confidence that he has exercised his franchise. 7. **Sense of belonging:** It gives the voter a sense of belonging to the political process. **Demerits of Secret Balloting** 1. **It is difficult to comprehend:** This type or system is difficult because of the tedious process the voter has to go through before voting. 2. **Illiteracy:** The illiterate ones can vote blindly because they cannot read or write. 3. **It is time consuming:** Many people might decide not to vote because it is time-consuming. 4. **Costly to operate:** The cost involved in the production of the materials needed for the election may be so high. **Public Voting:** It is the type of voting that is done openly or publicly. The voters will have to line up according to their political parties and the counting of the voters is done by the presiding officer before everybody present. The results are declared on the spot. Nigeria has tried this system in the past. **Merits of Public Voting** 1. **Simple to operate:** The voting exercise is very simple to operate. 2. **Less costly:** It is less costly to operate. Not as costly as secret ballot. 3. **Public responsibility:** As voting is a public responsibility, its exercise should also be public. 4. **Suffrage is a trust:** Suffrage is a trust. Every eligible adult is entitled to vote during an election. 5. **Sufficient enlightenment:** If a community is adequately enlightened and members are courageous enough to withstand improper pressure, public voting should be desirable. 6. **It reduces electoral malpractices:** This is possibly reduced because the materials that can enhance electoral malpractices are not in place, e.g. ballot box, ballot papers, etc. 7. **Immediate release of results:** The voters are counted on the spot and the results declared before everybody. **Demerits of Public Voting** 1. **Coercion of the voters:** The voter may be compelled to vote for a particular person, whose pressure he may not be able to withstand. 2. **Pressures from government:** Government can exert pressure upon the electorate to vote for government officials. 3. **Landlords and employers:** They can equally exert pressure on those who are more or less subject to their control. 4. **Exposure to intimidation, etc.:** Voters are exposed to intimidation, harassment, etc. and as such, many may keep away from voting. 5. **It is crude:** Open ballot is seen as crude and uncivilised. It is unacceptable to a modern political system. 6. **It can induce violence:** Those not satisfied with the outcome of the result may go on rampage and destruction of lives and property could result from the process. 7. **Manipulation of the results:** Electoral officials could still manipulate the results of the election, by declaration of false figures. **13.7 INDIRECT ELECTION** In this system, the electorate (voters) elect the members of parliament (law-makers) and the law-makers will have to vote on behalf of the people to elect the president, i.e. the head of state of the country. This system was used in the first Republic to elect the first President of Nigeria – 1963-66. Nnamdi Azikiwe was the first President of Nigeria. However, this system is mostly operational in a parliamentary system of government. Great Britain is a good example. **Merits of Indirect Election** 1. **Best qualified to vote:** The legislators form an electoral college in an indirect election. The possible advantage is that members of the college are seen as best qualified to elect leaders. 2. **Less violent:** An indirect election produces less violence than a direct election. 3. **They are well informed:** Members of the electoral college have the opportunity of examining the candidates before the voting. 4. **Rigging:** The opportunity of rigging is partially absent. It may or may not be there. 5. **Less costly:** An indirect election is not as costly as a direct election. **Demerits of Indirect Election** 1. **Undemocratic:** The electorate are not directly involved in the final choice of their representatives. 2. **No room for wider political participation:** An indirect election system does not offer wider political participation in the state. 3. **Bribery and corruption:** It promotes bribery and corruption and manipulation of delegates. 4. **People’s will may be jeopardised:** Final result may not reflect the will of the people. 5. **Denial of the people’s popular sovereignty:** The system denies the people of the popular sovereignty. The electorate have the ultimate power to elect candidates of their choice. 6. **Violation of the principle of one man, one vote:** An indirect election violates the democratic principle of one man one vote. 8. **Discontent:** It may breed discontent and political instability in the system. 9. **Political apathy:** The people may be apathetic to vote in subsequent elections. 10. **Imposition:** The final political office holders voted in by the representatives seem to be an imposition on the people. 11. **Corrupt electoral officials:** There is the possibility of the electoral officials getting involved in some corrupt practices during the process of election. **13.8 ELECTORAL COLLEGE** **Meaning:** This is a group or body of elected representatives who in turn come together to elect people into political offices. It is also a group of people who are chosen to represent the members of a political party in the election of a leader. The Americans elect their President through Electoral College. **Merits of Electoral College** 1. **Electoral malpractice:** This is minimised because only a few people vote. 2. **Short period of voting:** Voting could be done and completed within a very short period and on time too. 3. **Easy and proper identification of candidates:** The candidates are easily identified and properly known to the voters. 4. **Clear-cut winner:** The system produces a winner easily because the candidate scores with a simple majority. 5. **Less costly:** The system is not costly but simple to operate. **Demerits of Electoral College** 1. **People’s right of choice are denied them:** This is because they did not take part in the second election. 2. **Wrong candidate:** The system might not produce the right candidate. 3. **Bribery and corruption:** It can lead to bribery and corruption and which will influence the outcome of the election. 4. **The legislators do not possess enviable qualities:** The legislators, forming the body called electoral college, portray themselves as a body possessing enviable and distinct qualities for electing candidates, whereas they may not be. **13.9 OTHER TERMS ASSOCIATED WITH ELECTION** **(1) Bye-election:** This is an election that takes place to fill a vacant elective post as a result of disqualification, resignation or death of the individual holding that office. The election takes place in that constituency, where there has been a vacancy. Therefore, only the registered voters in the constituency are allowed to vote. **(2) Run-off election or second ballot:** When none of the candidates wins the election by absolute majority in a general election, another election would be conducted. In this final election, only the candidates with the highest votes are allowed to contest. **(3) Primary election:** This involves the political parties presenting candidates for any election in a country. It is an election conducted within a political party to choose credible candidate(s) that can adequately represent the interests of the party in any election. **(4) Referendum:** This is a ‘yes’ or ‘no’ vote of the people particularly on law in a given political system. Referendum ensures that laws not in conformity with the popular will are not passed. This is a useful check on the powers of the legislature. **(5) Plebiscite:** This is also a ‘yes’ or ‘no’ vote of the people, especially on issues of national importance referred to them. It can also be on some relevant public or political questions like the issues of minorities in a political set-up. **(6) The process of recall:** It means the “calling back” of elected law-makers, executive officers or judges before the expiration of the period for which they were elected. Following immediately is the election of others to fill the places left vacant. This system is common in most American states. **13.10 FREE AND FAIR ELECTION** **Meaning:** Free and fair election is an election conducted according to the principles and rules of democracy. This simply means that the people of a given country must be free, without coercion, to elect their leaders under a condition that is most favourable and conducive. In addition, every section of the country must be fully represented in the legislature. **Conditions Necessary for the Conduct of a Free and Fair Election** 1. **Division into constituencies:** The country must be divided into constituencies based on population with regard to the number of people needed to constitute the parliament. 2. **Preparation of voters register:** Only qualified citizens should be registered and allowed to vote. 3. **Secret balloting:** Voting should be done secretly, so as to give the voters the freedom to vote for candidates of their choice. The principle of one man, one vote must be respected. 4. **Intimidation of voters:** Voters should not be intimidated at the polls. They should be free to exercise their franchise. 5. **Impartiality of electoral officers:** They should not collaborate either with the government or any political party to jeopardise free and fair voting. 6. **Independence of electoral commission:** Government should not interfere with the conduct of the elections. 7. **Counting of votes:** Votes must be counted under strict supervision to avoid rigging of elections. Results must be declared immediately after count. 8. **Opportunity to campaign:** Candidates must be given adequate time and opportunity to campaign and present their manifestos to the electorate. 9. **Access to court:** An aggrieved candidate should have access to a court of law to challenge the result of the election. 10. **Political education of the electorate:** The electorate should be educated on their rights and how to identify party symbols and the proper manner of voting. 11. **Voting by proxy:** There should be no voting by proxy, that is, voting on behalf of another person. Also, there should be no double registration. 12. **Training of officials and provision of election materials:** Electoral officials should be given adequate and sufficient training and enough materials needed for every election. 13. **Revision of voters’ register:** This is necessary before any election. This is to make enough room for claims and objections. 14. **Voters’ list:** There should be regular, periodic and public display of voters’ list. 15. **Periodic elections:** The electoral commission should make sure that elections are conducted periodically and regular too. 16. **Qualification for election:** The requirement for qualification for election must be clearly stipulated and adequately publicised. 17. **Security:** Before and during elections, all election materials should be thoroughly secured. 18. **The media:** All candidates for election should have equal access to the media. No candidate should be deprived of this opportunity. 19. **Independent judiciary:** There should be in existence independent and impartial judiciary. 20. **Party system:** There should also be in existence a multi-party system. This will make for a better representation of the people in government. **13.11 ELECTORAL COMMISSION** **Definition:** Electoral commission is a body responsible for organising and conducting elections in a political system. It can also be seen as an independent and impartial body responsible for conducting and organising all elections in a given country. It has a chairman as the head and other members of the commission. In Nigeria, for example, FEDECO was the commission responsible for 1979/83 Second Republic elections; NEC was responsible for the conduct of Third Republic’s election which was aborted while INEC conducted the 1998/99 general elections for the Fourth Republic. INEC also conducted the 2003, 2007, 2011, 2015 and 2019 general elections of the Fourth Republic. **Features of an Electoral Commission** 1. **Organising and conducting elections:** It is a body responsible for organising and conducting all elections in a political system. 2. **Free and fair elections:** The commission is responsible for the conduct of free and fair elections in a country. 3. **Announcement of election results:** It has the sole power of announcing election results. 4. **Independent body:** It is an independent and impartial electoral body. 5. **Controlled and headed by a chairman:** The commission is controlled by the chairman while other members are appointed. 6. **Fixed tenure of office:** The members of this commission have a fixed tenure of office. 7. **Resident commissioner:** There are resident commissioners for each state of the federation. 8. **A neutral body:** It is a neutral body and should be non-partisan in outlook. 9. **It is guided by the constitution:** This commission has the backing of the law in all its dispensation and actions. 10. **Honesty and fair play:** The commission should be honest and just in its decision-making especially with the conduct of the elections. **Functions of Electoral Commission** 1. **Registration of parties:** It registers political parties according to laid down rules and regulations. 2. **Free and fair elections:** The electoral commission has the sole power to organise and conduct free and fair elections in a country. 3. **Registration of voters:** Eligible voters are registered by the commission. 4. **Provision of electoral materials:** This body has to provide all the necessary materials needed for any elections, for example, ballot boxes, ballot papers, polling booths, etc. 5. **Delimitation (division) of the country:** It divides the country into constituencies or electoral districts. 6. **Appointment and training:** It does the appointment and training of electoral officers, for instance, presiding officers, clerks and polling orderlies. 7. **Counting of votes:** The commission is responsible for the counting of votes and releasing the result of each election conducted. 8. **Financial grants to parties:** The commission gives financial grants to political parties. 9. **Enlightenment:** The commission educates the electorate about the necessary issues involved in elections. 10. **Types of voting:** It informs the people the type of voting to be conducted, either secret or open (public) voting. 11. **Screening of candidates:** Candidates for election are properly screened and their eligibility ascertained before any election. 12. **Security at the polling stations:** Adequate security is provided at the polling stations by the commission. **Officials Used in the Conduct of Election** (i) Chairman/Electoral Commissioners. (ii) Resident/Regional/State/Electoral Officers. (iii) Returning Officers. (iv) Presiding Officers. (v) Polling Assistants/Clerks. (vi) Polling Agents/Party Agents. (vii) Security Officers/Polling Orderlies. (viii) Election Observers. (ix) Supervisory/Presiding Officers. **Functions of the Officials** (i) **Chairman/Electoral Commissioners:** They move around to supervise the conduct of elections. (ii) **Resident/regional/state/electoral officers:** They supervise the conduct of elections at the state/regional levels. (iii) **Returning officers:** They announce results and declare winners at polling units and wards. (iv) **Presiding assistant/clerks:** They assist the presiding officer by checking the list of registered voters. (vi) **Polling agents/party agents:** They represent the candidates or political parties at the polling booths/stations. (vii) **Security officers/polling orderlies:** They maintain law and order at the polling stations/centres. (viii) **Election observers:** They monitor the conduct of elections and report on the freeness or otherwise of the elections. **(ix) Supervisory/ Presiding officers:** They move from one polling centre to the other within the electoral wards, listening to the complaints and settling minor disagreements arising from the polling units/ centres. **Revision Questions** 1.
