### **18. PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION 3: LOCAL GOVERNMENT** **18.1 PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES** At the end of this chapter, students should be able to: (1) Explain the meaning of local government. (2) Gives the reasons for the creation of local governments. (3) List the functions performed by local governments. (4) Mention the various types of local government. (5) Discuss the evolution of local government in Nigeria. (6) Describe the structure of local government. (7) Explain how local governments are financed. (8) Explain the relationship between the government authorities and the state/ federal governments. (9) Explain the roles of traditional rulers in local government administration. (10) Discuss the reforms of local government. (11) Suggest the measures to improve local government administration. **18.2 LOCAL GOVERNMENT** **Definition:** Local government may be defined as a government established through an Act of Parliament at the local level and to deal with specific matters as it affects them. Under the 1963 Republican Constitution, local government authorities were created mainly to deal with matters of local concern, e.g. markets, feeder roads and motor parks. The essence of local government creation is to involve local participation in the affairs of their country. Not only that, it is meant to bring them into the realms of politics at the grassroots. Most modern states and governments embrace three or two tiers of government, for example, central, state and local governments, as we have in Nigeria. The powers of government and administration are therefore made relevant at the local level. Local governments are created through a document called ‘Instrument’. They are created like corporate bodies because they had legal existence. They could sue and be sued. **18.3 POWERS OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** With the creation of local government through an Act of Parliament, the following powers can be exercised. 1. **Making of bye-laws:** The legislative arm of local governments involved in making of bye-laws. The essence and effect of these laws are only felt within the local government concern. 2. **Power to punish offenders:** They can punish those who violate the laws made. They are tried in customary courts within the set-up. 3. **Imposition of levies:** They can impose levies on the people for some specific purposes. **18.4 REASONS FOR THE CREATION OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Bringing government nearer to the people:** This is done, so that those at the grassroots will be part of the government. This system is good for a large country like Nigeria. 2. **Local participation:** This is to provide opportunities for the local people to participate in the politics of their country. 3. **Even development:** Creation of local governments provided the avenue for even development of all parts of the country. 4. **Diverse interests:** The interests of the local people differ, and to cater for these interests, there must be the creation of local governments. 5. **Agents of central and state governments:** Local governments act as agents of central and state governments in the implementation of policies, e.g. maintenance of law and order. 6. **Provision of essential services:** The creation is also to complement the efforts of central and state governments in the provision of essential services to the local populace. 7. **Employment:** They can also provide employment at the local level. 8. **Community development:** They can equally provide the opportunity for local participation in community development projects. 9. **Training and development of future political leaders:** These are made possible at the grassroots by the creation of local governments. 10. **Sense of belonging:** One of the reasons is to give the local people a sense of belonging, for example, in resolving local matters as they affects them. 11. **Decentralisation of power:** This brought about having three (3) tiers of government with residual power going to the local governments. 12. **To mobilise:** Human and material resources at the local level for rapid development. 13. **To serve as a link:** Between the central government and local areas. 14. **To be involved:** In the preservation of traditional institutions in local areas. 15. **Bye-laws:** To make and enforce bye-laws, The legislative arm of local government is involved in this matter. 16. **Maintenance of law and order:** One of the reasons is to involve the local governments in the maintenance of law and order at the local level. **18.5 FUNCTIONS OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Provision of health centres:** This is done for the people at the grassroots. For example, they provide and possibly maintain maternity centres and dispensaries. 2. **Provision of water:** They at times construct boreholes, wells, etc. for the people. 3. **Naming of streets:** They are involved in the naming of streets and roads. They also number houses. 4. **Maintenance of some institutions:** They can establish, maintain and regulate slaughter houses, markets, motor parks and public convenience. 5. **Maintenance of roads:** They can construct new feeder roads and the maintenance of roads under their jurisdiction. 6. **Collection of rates, radio and television licences:** This is part of their functions. 7. **Registration:** Local governments are involved in the registration of births, deaths and marriages in their areas of authority. 8. **Establishment and maintenance of cemeteries and homes of destinies:** Local governments are equally involved in these two functions. 9. **Formulation of policies:** They are involved in the formulation of economic planning and development schemes for the local government areas. 10. **Management of primary education:** Originally, local governments are responsible for the funding and management of primary education. 11. **Maintenance of law and order:** The making of bye-laws is to enhance the maintenance of law and order in local government. 12. **Provision of transport services:** Most local governments provide transport services as a means of generating more revenue for the local governments. 13. **Provision of recreational centres:** Local governments provide and maintain most of these centres like amusement parks, stadia, etc. for relaxation purposes. **18.6 SOURCES OF REVENUE FOR LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Allocation from the federation accounts:** Local governments receive the bulk of their money from the allocation of the federation accounts. 2. **Grants:** Another source of revenue is the special grants they receive from both the central and state governments. However, in most cases, these grants are for specific projects. 3. **Imposition of levies:** They also generate funds through imposition of levies on the people. The levy may be for a special project, meant to improve the welfare of the people. 4. **Collection of rates:** Revenues are equally generated through collection of rates on radio and television, market shops, motor parks, etc. 5. **Commercial ventures:** In recent times, some local governments are involved in commercial ventures like, transportation, recreational centres, etc. 6. **Defaulters of bye-laws:** Fines are paid by those that have offended the rules and regulations and this forms part of the local government revenue. 7. **States allocation:** In Nigeria, at least 10% of internally generated revenue is from state government. 8. **Court fines:** They form part of the sharing of court fines in Nigeria. 9. **Donations and gifts:** From Non-governmental Organisations (NGOs), citizens, etc., form part of revenue for local government. 10. **Royalties:** Royalties from companies e.g. in Ghana, Sierra-Leone and The Gambia. **18.7 RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN STATE/FEDERAL AND LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Allocation from the federation account:** The local governments receive their money from the federation account, involving the central and state governments as well. 2. **Local government are created through an Act of parliament:** The local governments are created with specific powers which can be withdrawn by the central government, if not performed within the confines of the law of the land. 3. **Grants:** They receive grants from both the central and state governments. They can equally suspend releasing the grants to them for some reasons, e.g. misappropriation of funds. 4. **Enactment of bye-laws:** Most of the bye-laws made, may be rejected by the parliament. Not only that, the judiciary can declare such laws unconstitutional. 5. **Dissolution of local government:** The state government has the power to dissolve any local government. This means that the chairman and councillors should vacate their offices. 6. **Approval of loans:** Approval of loans for local government, in any financial institutions, may have to come from the state government. 7. **Auditing:** The state or central government may send auditors to audit the finances of the local governments, to enhance sound financial management. 8. **Amendment of laws:** The National Assembly, through the central government, may at any time decide to amend the laws establishing local governments. **18.8 CONTROL OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Dissolution of local government:** The state government has the power to dissolve any local government. When this happens, the chairman and councillors should then vacate their offices on dissolution of the council. 2. **Approval of loans:** Any loan to be taken by the local government from any financial institution must get the approval of the state government. 3. **Auditing:** Auditors should be appointed by either the state or central government to audit the finances of the local government. 4. **Specific functions:** The state House of Assembly can ask a local government to perform certain specific functions. 5. **Amendment of laws:** The National Assembly, through the central government, can at any time decide to amend the laws establishing local governments. 6. **Withholding of grants:** The central government may withhold local government grants if implicated in financial mismanagement. 7. **Monitoring the activities of local government:** The state government can do this by ensuring that they adhere to guidelines and rules set for them. 8. **Judicial control:** The courts can declare any bye-law made null and void or unconstitutional, if such laws are not in line with the provisions of the country’s constitution. 9. **Public control:** People are free to criticise some of the activities of local governments and can come up with useful suggestions that would help in sanitising some of these activities. **Ways by which the Central Government can Improve upon Local Government System in West Africa** 1. Increasing central government grants to local authorities for developmental projects. 2. The Ministry of Local Government should evolve an action plan for local authorities to generate revenue internally. 3. Minimising central government interference and partisanship in local government administration. 4. Improvement of service conditions of local government workers in order to attract highly skilled personnel. 5. Providing funds to organise refresher courses and in-service training for workers. 6. Effective internal and external auditing of accounts. 7. Effective ministerial control to avoid misappropriation and misapplication as well as misallocation of funds. 8. Provision of adequate logistics for local authorities, e.g. vehicles, furniture and computer facilities. 9. Assembly/Council members should be democratically elected to serve the interest of the people. 10. Transparent appointment of local government administrators. 11. Application of technology in the operations of the local government system. 12. Granting of financial autonomy to the local government unit. 13. The central government should scrutinise all loans contracted by local governments. **18.9 IMPORTANCE OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS IN A STATE** 1. **Political education:** Local governments help in educating people at the grassroots about the policies and actions of the federal and state governments. 2. **Preserving the traditional institutions:** They help in preserving the traditional institutions of the people in the local areas. 3. **Government at the grassroots:** They bring government nearer to the people at the grassroots. 4. **Feelings of insecurity:** Local government allays feelings of insecurity and fears of ethnic domination especially if created along ethnic lines. 5. **Human and material resources:** They mobilise both human and material resources for community development. 6. **Serve as a link:** They serve as a link between the people at the grassroots and the central/state governments. 7. **Provision of social services:** Local governments provide social services to the people at the grassroots. 8. **Leadership training:** They encourage leadership training of the people at the grassroots. 9. **Development:** They bring rapid development to the local areas. 10. **Governance:** Local governments allow the indigenes of different local areas to govern themselves according to their traditions and cultures. 11. **Law and order:** They help in the maintenance of law and order. 12. **Quick decision:** Local governments facilitate quick decision-making in matters within their jurisdiction. 13. **Making of bye-laws:** Local governments make bye-laws for order and good governance of their areas. 14. **Job opportunities:** They help to create job opportunities in local areas. **18.10 PROBLEMS OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Shortage of trained personnel:** Acute shortage of trained and experienced personnel affect the efficiency of local governments. 2. **Acute shortage of fund:** Most local governments do not have enough funds with which to operate. They have enormous responsibilities before them but they lack fund. 3. **Political interference:** Both the military and civilian governments have at different times interfered with the activities of the local government. For example, state governments often deny them the required encouragement to work effectively. 4. **Bribery and corruption:** Some officials demand and even take bribe before performing their official functions. 5. **Embezzlement of public fund:** Some of the officials involved in revenue collection embezzle part of this fund for their own selfish ends. 6. **Granting of undue favour:** Some local government officials use their positions to give undue favour to their friends, for example, in the award of contracts. 7. **Political victimisation:** Most local government are victimised politically, and frustrated, especially if they are not in the good books of the ruling political party. 8. **Tribalism, nepotism and favouritism:** These are the order of the day in terms of appointment, transfer, discipline and promotion of staff. 9. **Low incentive:** The incentive to work is low, leading to poor attitude to work of local government staff. 10. **Inappropriate structure:** Some local governments in Nigeria are too small or too large in size. 11. **Revenue collection:** An inefficient method of revenue collection is one of the problems confronting local governments in Nigeria. 12. **Boundary disputes:** These exist among some local governments, resulting in clashes and deaths in recent times. **18.11 SUGGESTED SOLUTIONS TO THE PROBLEMS OF LOCAL GOVERNMENTS** 1. **Improvement on method of revenue generation:** The method of revenue generation has to be overhauled. Honest and capable hands should be used in the collection of rates and other levies. 2. **In-service training:** Most of the workers should be given the opportunity to further their training in local government administration. 3. **Provision of enough fund:** Enough fund should be provided to the local governments. This would help to tackle most of the responsibilities facing them, for example in the provision of infrastructural facilities such as roads, library services and electricity. 4. **Allocation from federal accounts to local governments:** The government should increase the allocation from federal revenue accounts to local governments. This will help to boost their financial bases. 5. **Discouragement of tribalism, nepotism and favouritism:** These should be discouraged, and recruitment of workers into local governments should be based on merits. 6. **Provision of grants:** Grants (from state and central governments) to local governments should be enough and regular too. 7. **Auditing:** Auditing should be done regularly in order to enhance efficient and sound financial managements. 8. **Easy access to loans:** Access to loans should not be made difficult for local governments. 9. **The activities of local government:** Their activities should not be politically motivated. 10. **More avenues for revenue generation:** They should go into more commercial ventures in order to improve on their financial bases. **Revision Questions** 1.
