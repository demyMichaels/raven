### **15. MASS MEDIA** **15.1 PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES** At the end of this chapter, students should be able to: (1) Explain the meaning of mass media. (2) Discuss the roles of mass media in the day-to-day running of Government. (3) State the impact of mass media in Nigerian politics. **15.2 MASS MEDIA** **Definition:** It is the totality of means of communication within a society, state, etc. such as television, film production, radio, newspaper, magazine and the Internet including newsletter in which messages, news and information get across the population, government and its agencies. Mass media also stands as a means of entertaining, informing and instructing in a pleasurable manner. **15.3 ROLES OF MASS MEDIA IN THE DAY-TO-DAY RUNNING OF GOVERNMENT** 1. **It disseminates information:** The mass media informs the people about the activities of political parties, their programme and preparations towards any political election in the country. 2. **Education:** The mass media educates the populace on government activities, for example, in the areas of education, roads construction, transportation and health. 3. **It enlightens the people on the activities of some organisations.** For example, oil companies can use the mass media to enlighten the people on their position on youth restiveness in the Niger Delta region. Secondly, the plans to reduce environmental pollution in the area. Thirdly, it also enlightens the people on government’s amnesty plan for repentant militants in the Niger Delta area. 4. **Social responsibility:** Mass media also reports some organisations’ corporate social responsibility development programmes and projects to improve the standard of living of their host communities, e.g. some oil companies. 5. **Two-way channel:** It serves as a two-way channel of communication between the government and the people. 6. **Accountability:** It enables the people to hold their government accountable especially in the areas they have failed in their campaign promises. Governance is all about accountability. Government should be able to give account of their stewardship to the people, even while in office. For example, Lagos State government has been demonstrating this since 1999 to date. 7. **Security challenges:** The mass media is playing a crucial role in this area. It informs the public about the areas in the country where lives and property are threatened. 8. **Employment opportunities:** It provides employment opportunities to a large number of people such as editors, script writers, pressmen, etc. 9. **Revenue for the state through the payment of taxes.** 10. **Feedback:** Mass media provides feedback to government on its policies. 11. **Education:** It educates the people about their rights, duties and obligation towards the state. 12. **Instrument for change:** Mass media serves as an instrument for change in society, e.g. fighting corruption and preaching morality. 13. **Human rights:** It serves as an instrument for protecting and promoting the fundamental Human Rights of the people. 14. **Medium of national issues:** It serves as a medium through which important national issues can be raised and debated and solutions provided. 15. **Provision of a forum for criticism:** It provides a forum for making constructive criticisms on government policies. 16. **Expression of opinion:** Mass media offers an avenue for expression of opinion by the public on both national and international issues. 17. **National development:** It mobilises the people towards national development. 18. **Political participation:** People are mobilised for political participation. **15.4 THE IMPACT OF MASS MEDIA IN NIGERIAN POLITICS/ WATCHDOG** 1. **Choice of political parties:** Through the mass media, choice of political parties is always made possible. 2. **Popularising political parties:** Through T.V. advertising, political parties are made popular by impacting negatively or positively in the minds of the populace on political issues. 3. **Learner’s interest:** Mass media generally stands in the position of sustaining the leaner’s interest on any subject. 4. **Focusing and directing:** The media focuses and directs the individual’s attention towards a particular objective with a singular goal of enhancing behavioural change. 5. **Instructional media:** It has been found to occupy a prominent position in assisting individual learners to accomplish a desired level of competence. 6. **Generation of people’s interest:** One of the impacts of mass media is the generation of people’s interest for popular political participation in government. 7. **People’s mouthpiece:** The mass media developed and is now the people’s mouthpiece on issues affecting them socially, politically and economically. 8. **Accountability:** People can now hold the government accountable for their deplorable state in life and the government’s unconcerned and uncaring attitude towards their welfare generally. 9. **Awakening people’s feelings:** Another impact is the awakening of the people’s feelings to fight for their political and civic rights. 10. **Political education:** Another effect is that the people now have negative and positive feelings about their government and style of leadership. **Types of Media** In education, instructional materials, media and educational media are used interchangeably. The following are the basic classification of instructional materials: 1. **Visuals:** Visuals appeal to the sense of sight only – charts, maps, globes, etc. 2. **Audios:** Audios appeal to sense of sound only – radio, recorded tapes, telephones, talking drums, audio compact disc, etc. 3. **Audio-visuals:** Audio-visuals appeal to sense of sound and sight, television, video, video CD, DVD, computer, etc. **Mass Media – as the Watchdog** The media is the people’s watchdog. The media as the Fourth Estate of the Realm watches over the activities of government on behalf of the people. It reports to the people all the activities of government at all levels of governance – local, state and federal governments, thereby exacting much political influence. The mass media also informs and reminds the government of neglect to issues deeply affecting the people, for example, deplorable roads, lack of portable water, alarming rate of poverty, violation of people’s right and insecurity of lives and property across the country. **Revision Questions** 1. What is mass media? 2. (a) Describe the impact of mass media in Nigerian politics. (b) Outline the types of media in Nigeria. 3. Identify any five roles performed by the mass media in nation building. (WASSCE June, 2016).
