Drone Stabilization Module

Control a drone whose altitude is modeled by a polynomial function. Divide by the controller's transfer function to stabilize it.

Divide: \(\frac{2x^3 + 5x^2 - 9x - 18}{-x^2 - x + 6}\)

Simplify to find the stable control function.

Polynomial Division Steps:

Step 1: Rewrite the divisor with a leading coefficient of 1: \(\frac{2x^3 + 5x^2 - 9x - 18}{-x^2 - x + 6} = \frac{2x^3 + 5x^2 - 9x - 18}{-1(x^2 + x - 6)}\)

Step 2: Divide \(2x^3\) by \(-x^2\) to get \(-2x\) as the first term of the quotient

Step 3: Multiply \(-2x\) by \(-x^2 - x + 6\) to get \(2x^3 + 2x^2 - 12x\)

Step 4: Subtract from the dividend: \((2x^3 + 5x^2 - 9x - 18) - (2x^3 + 2x^2 - 12x) = 3x^2 + 3x - 18\)

Step 5: Divide \(3x^2\) by \(-x^2\) to get \(-3\) as the next term

Step 6: Multiply \(-3\) by \(-x^2 - x + 6\) to get \(3x^2 + 3x - 18\)

Step 7: Subtract: \((3x^2 + 3x - 18) - (3x^2 + 3x - 18) = 0\)

Step 8: Since the remainder is 0, the quotient is \(-2x - 3\)
