Module 1: Objectives of Departmental Accounts 🎯

Businesses divide accounting into departments to:

Track Performance

See which departments are performing well or need improvement

Evaluate Managers

Assess department managers' effectiveness

Identify Profitability

Find which products or services make the most money

Strategic Planning

Make decisions about expanding or reducing departments

Example: Fashion Store 👕

A clothing store might have departments for:

👔
Men's Clothing
👗
Women's Clothing
👟

👜

By tracking each department separately, the store can see which is most profitable and allocate resources accordingly.

Module 2: Apportionment of Expenses 💰

Some expenses benefit multiple departments and need to be apportionedDivided or allocated among departments based on a reasonable basis:

Direct Expenses

Directly identifiable with a specific department:

  • Department-specific staff salaries
  • Department advertising
  • Department manager's salary
  • Department-specific supplies

Indirect Expenses

Shared expenses that must be allocated:

  • Rent and rates
  • Insurance
  • Electricity and heating
  • General administration costs

Common Bases for Apportionment

Floor Area
For rent, rates, building insurance, heating
Number of Employees
For staff welfare, canteen expenses
Sales Value
For sales commission, delivery expenses
Weight/Volume
For shipping, handling costs
Electricity Usage
For electricity costs
Capital Value
For depreciation, insurance of assets

Module 3: Departmental Trading and P&L Account 📝

Departmental accounts show the trading results of each department separately:

Trading Account

Shows gross profitSales revenue minus cost of goods sold for each department:

  • Sales
  • Less: Cost of goods sold
  • = Gross profit

Profit & Loss Account

Shows net profitGross profit minus operating expenses for each department:

  • Gross profit
  • Less: Direct expenses
  • Less: Apportioned expenses
  • = Net profit

Key Components

Column Format

Each department gets its own column, with a total column:

Profitability Analysis

Key ratios to assess performance:

  • Gross profit margin(Gross profit ÷ Sales) × 100%
  • Net profit margin(Net profit ÷ Sales) × 100%
  • Return on capital employed(Net profit ÷ Capital employed) × 100%
