The Birth of Double-Entry Bookkeeping

+ 91
+ 92

1494: Luca Pacioli's Contribution

+ 93

+ 94 Italian mathematician Luca Pacioli published "Summa de Arithmetica", which included the first published description of double-entry bookkeeping. This system, already used by Venetian merchants, became the foundation of modern accounting. + 95

+ 96
+ 97

Key Principle:

+ 98

Every transaction affects at least two accounts: a debit and a credit, ensuring the accounting equation always balances.

+ 99
+ 100
+ 101

+ 102 This revolutionary system allowed merchants to track assets, liabilities, and capital more accurately, facilitating the growth of commerce during the Renaissance. + 103
