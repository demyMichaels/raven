Tubers

Yam, Cassava, Sweet Potato

Learning Objectives

  • Identify common and scientific names of major tuber crops
  • Describe the gross morphology and anatomy of storage organs
  • Explain methods of propagation for each tuber crop
  • Outline husbandry practices, harvesting, processing and storage
  • Recognize common diseases, pests, and their effects on yield
  • Determine the economic importance of tuber crops
  • Relate their importance to national economic development

Tubers are swollen underground storage organs (modified stems or roots) that store carbohydrates. They are staple foods in West Africa and provide food security for millions. The major tuber crops include yam, cassava, and sweet potato.

🍠 Yam

Dioscorea spp.

King of crops in West Africa. Important in culture and ceremonies.

  • Family: Dioscoreaceae
  • Propagation: Yam sets (whole or cut tubers)
  • Maturity: 6–10 months
  • Main products: Boiled yam, pounded yam, fried yam
View Details →

🍠 Cassava

Manihot esculenta

Drought-tolerant staple; Nigeria is world's largest producer.

  • Family: Euphorbiaceae
  • Propagation: Stem cuttings (20-30 cm)
  • Maturity: 6–24 months
  • Main products: Garri, fufu, lafun, flour, starch
View Details →

🍠 Sweet Potato

Ipomoea batatas

Nutritious, fast-maturing crop grown for its sweet tubers and leaves.

  • Family: Convolvulaceae
  • Propagation: Vine cuttings (slips)
  • Maturity: 3–5 months
  • Main products: Boiled, fried, flour, animal feed

💰 Economic Importance of Tubers

Food Security

  • Staple foods for millions in West Africa
  • Rich source of carbohydrates
  • Can be stored in-ground (cassava) or in barns (yam)

Employment

  • Provides livelihoods for farmers, processors, traders
  • Women heavily involved in processing

Industrial Uses

  • Cassava starch for textiles, pharmaceuticals
  • Yam flour for baking
  • Sweet potato for animal feed

Export Earnings

  • Yam exported to Europe and USA
  • Cassava chips and pellets exported for animal feed
